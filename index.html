About Us

Chitkara ACM Student chapter is a student organization affiliated with the Association of Applied Technology (ACM). Our goal is to create a powerful platform for students to connect, learn and exchange knowledge. We organize various events such as technical training, non-technical training and entertainment. ACM student chapters play an important role in fostering communication and collaboration within the computer science community and expanding these connections to the broader computing community.

Top Events

Codathon 2k24

Codathon 2k24 was the event in which students Embrace the coding extravaganza for thrilling problem-solving, collaboration, and creativity. It does not matter you are a coding wizard or a novice explorer, face challenges that push boundaries. Shine in the spirit of innovation. All were Ready to code their way to victory and Secure your spot now and embark on a coding adventure like never before! It was mesmerising event full of coding buzz.

Design O Tech.

The DESIGN-OTECH 2.0, held on September 26, 20203 at Chitkara University Einstein Hall , was a resounding success, uniting creative minds, tech enthusiasts and innovative thinkers. Participants took on Hackathon Challenges, enhancing HTML codes with CSS to transform provided content. Teams collaborated across diverse backgrounds, yielding innovative designs.

Hour Of Code

"Hour of Code," the ACM's annual event, unfolded with a burst of excitement at 10 am. Kicking off with a lively balloon-bursting game, participants dove into a captivating drag-and-drop challenge, uploading certificates for analysis.The event concluded within the stipulated boundaries, leaving happy faces and a sense of accomplishment.

CodeCrafters

Code Crafters 2k23 was an event held at Chitkara University on April 12, 2023. With the "Critical Thinking for Innovative Solutions to Overcome Problems," it aimed to foster coding skills among students. The event provided a platform for students to showcase their coding abilities while promoting camaraderie among participants. It featured challenging coding rounds where students could demonstrate their critical thinking and problem-solving skills.
